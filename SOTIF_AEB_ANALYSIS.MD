SOTIF TRIGGERING CONDITION ANALYSIS
SYSTEM: AUTOMATIC EMERGNECY BRAKING(AEB)
STANDARD:- ISO 21448
AUTHOR:- MSc MECHATRONICS- UNIVERSITY OF EAST LONDON

SOTIF VS ISO 26262-KEY DIFFERENCE:-
ISO 26262 ADDRESSES RANDOM HARDWARE FAILURES.
ISO 21448 (SOTIF) ADDRESSES PERFORMANCE LIMITATIONS AND MISUSE SCENARIOS WHERE HARWARE WORKS CORRECTLY
BUT THE SYSTEM STILL CAUSES HARM DUE TO SENSOR
LIMITATION OR UNEXPECTED ENVIRONMENTS

SOTIF TRIGGERING CONDITION 1- LOW LIGHT AND RAIN

SCENARIO:- VEHICLE OPERTING ON AN UNLIT URBAN ROAD IN HEAVY RAINFALL WITH STANDING WATER ON ROAD SURFACE.

TRIGGERING CONDITION: LOW AMBIENT LIGHT REDUCES CAMERA PIXEL CONTRAST BELOW THE OBJECT DETECTION THRESHOLD OF THE PERCEPTION ALGORITHM, CAUSING FALSE NEGATIVE OBSTACLE CLASSIFICATION.

HAZARD:- AEB SYSTEM FAILS TO ACTIVATE-VEHICLE COLLIDED WITH OBSTACLE AT SPEED.

MITIGATION: SENSOR FUSION- CROSS REFERENCE CAMERA DATA WITH RADAR WHICH IS LESS AFFECTED BY RAIN AND LOW LIGHT. IF CAMERA CONFIDENCE DROPS BELOW THRESHOLD, RADAR TAKES PRIMARY DETECTION ROLE.


SOTIF TRIGGERING CONDITION 2- MOTORWAY CONGESTION

SCENARIOS: HIGH DENSITY URBAN MOTORWAY DURING PEAK CONGESTION- M25 RUSH HOUR, LONDON

TRIGGERING CONDITION:- MULTIPLE OVERLAPPING BOUNDING BOXES IN THE OBJECT DETECTION MODEL CAUSE CLASSIFICATION CONFIDENCE TO DROP BELOW THE AEB ACTIVATION THRESHOLD,SYSTEM UNABLE TO DISTINGUISH STATTIONARY TRAFFIC FROM MOVING VEHICILES AT CLOSE RANGE.

HAZARD:- AEB ACTIVATES TOO LATE OR FAILS TO ACTIVATE-REAR END COLLISION AT MOTORWAY SPEED.

MITIGATION:- ADAPTIVE PROCESSING ALGORITHMS THAT INCREASE SENSOR SAMPLING FREQUENCY IN HIGH DENSITY TRAFFIC SCENARIOS. REDUNDANT RADAR PROVIDED DISTANCE MEASUREMENT INDEPENDENT OF VISUAL CLASSIFICATION.

SOTIF TRIGGERING CONDITION 3- SNOW AND FOG

SCENARIO:- HIGHLAND DRIVING IN WINTER CONDITIONS-SCOTLAND A9 CORRIDOR

TRIGGERING CONDITION: HEAVY SNOWFALL AND FREEZING FOG SIMULTANEOUSLY ON RADAR RANDOME REDUCED SIGNAL RETURN STRENGTH BY UPTO 40%, CAUSING FALSE NEGATIVE OBSTACLE DETECTION.

HAZARD:- AEB FAILS TOP DETECT OBSTACLE-COLLISION AT RURAL ROAD SPEED.

MITIGATION:- LIDAR AS THRID SENSING CHANNEL PROVIDING POINT CLOUD DETECTION INDEPENDENT OF OPTICAL CONDITIONS. SYSTEM ALERTS DRIVER TO TAKE MANUAL CONTROL WHEN ALL SENSOR CONFIDENCE DROPS BELOW SAFE THRESHOLD.

SOTIF TRIGGERING CONDITION 4- LOW SUN GLARE

SCENARIO : VEHICLE TRAVEELING WESTWARD DURING SUNSET ON A DUAL CARRIAGWAY-COMMOM UK EVENING COMMUTE.

TRIGGERING CONDITION; DIRECT SOLAR RADIATION AT LOW ANGLE SATURATES CAMERA IMAGE SENSOR CAUSING COMPLETE LOSS OF FORWARD OBEJCT DETECTION CAPABILITY.SUN GLARE ANGLE BETWEEN 0-15 DEGRESS CREATES MAXIMUM SENSOR SATURATION.

HAZARD:- AEB FAILS TO DETECT SLOW MOVING OR STATTIONARY VEHICLE AHEAD-HIGH SPEED REAR END COLLISION.

MITIGATION:- CAMERA EXPOSURE ALGORITHM AUTOMATICALLY ADJUSTS TO HIGH DYANMIC RANGE MODE DURING DETECTED GALRE CONDITIONS. RADAR MAINTAINS PRIMARY DETECTION ROLE WHEN CAMERA CONDIFIDENCE FALLS BELOW 60% THRESHOLD.

SOTIF TRIGGERING CONDITION 5- UNUSUAL ROAD OBSTACLES

SCENARIO:-RURAL RAOD ENCOUNTER WITH UNEXPECTED LARGE ANIMAL-COMMON IN SCOTTISH HIGHLAND AND WELSH COUNTRYSIDE

TRIGGERING CONDITIONS: AEB OBJECT CLASSIFICATION ALGORITHM TRAINED PREDOMINATLY ON VEHICLE AND PEDESTRAIN DATASETS FAILS TO CORRECTLY CLASSIFY LARGE ANIMAL SUCH  AS DEER OR LIVESTOCK AS OBSTCALES REQUIRING EMERGENCY BRAKING. CLASSIFICATION CONFIDENCE FALLS BLWO AEB ACTIVATION THRESHOLD.

HAZARD:- AEB FAILS TO ACTIVATE-VEHICLE COLLIDED WITH LARGE ANIMALS AT RURAL ROAD SPEED CAUSING SIGNIFICANT VEHICLE DAMAGE AND POTENTIAL OCCUPANT IN INJURY.

MITIGATION:- OBJECT CLASSIFICATION MODEL RETRAINED WITH DIVERSE OBSTACLE DATASETS INCLUDING ANIMALS AND UNUSUAL ROAD OBJECTS. ANY UNCLASSIFICED OBJECT ABOVE MINIMUM SIZE THRESHOLD TREATED AS POTENTIAL HAZARD TRIGGERING PRECAUTIONARY DECELRATION.

SUMMARY:- 

THIS ANALYSIS IDENTIFIES 5 SOTIF TRIGGERING CONDITIONS FOR AN AEB SYSTEM ACROSS DIFFERENT OPERATIONAL SCENARIOS. EACH CONDITION REPRESENTS A PERFORMANCE LIMITATION WHERE HARDWARE FUNCTIONS CORRECTLY BUT SYSTEM SAFETY CANNOT BE GUARENTEED DUE TO ENVIRONMENTAL OR SCENARIOS FACTORS OUTISDE THE SYSTEM DESIGN ENVELOPE.
